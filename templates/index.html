  • The STEAM Innovation Lab is essentially a hybrid makerspace where we invite NASA scientists, engineers and educators to explore and develop new ideas related to infusion of educational technology into STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics) activities, programs, and approaches.
  • By placing at least eight STEM exploration stations in close proximity, we encourage users to investigate how new and existing technologies can be effectively blended together for greater impact and integrated into existing education programs and products.
  • Stations currently include: Design and Fabrication {3D, Paper), Coding/Electronics, Mobile Sensores, AR/VR, Optics, Gamification/Virtual Environments, Mobile Media, Touchscreen Devices. Stations will be replaced with emerging technologies, as recommended by the HEC partners (i.e., ISTE, UNT).
  • We continually identify and incorporate new technologies as they become available into existing and new space science education programs
  • Through our partnership with UNT, we conduct short and long term evaluation and peer reviewed research on selected ideas produced in the lab.
  • Ideas collected in the lab are captured and shared with education networks in a variety of effective ways including video biogs, social media, monthly working group meetings and research. @NASASpaceSciEdu
  • We also work closely with ISTE to organize virtual workshops for informal educators that will allow them to explore and share emerging technologies.
  • Future ideas include replicating the lab or sections of the in other locations that will be called NASA Innovation Outposts.
